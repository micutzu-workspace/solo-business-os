# Inbound Triage System — Solo Business OS

## Goal
Handle inbound early-access interest in a structured way so signal quality can be evaluated without collecting unnecessary personal data or making ad-hoc decisions.

## Input source
Primary source for now:
- inbound emails sent to the early-access address

## What to record
Keep the internal record minimal:
- date received
- sender email
- language (RO/EN)
- source if known
- intent strength: weak / medium / strong
- buyer fit: low / medium / high
- key pain point mentioned
- action taken

## What not to record unless truly needed
- unnecessary personal details
- speculative profiling
- sensitive data unrelated to the product

## Signal scoring
### Strong signal
- clearly understands the product
- self-identifies as target buyer
- expresses desire to try or buy
- describes an operational pain point that matches the offer

### Medium signal
- seems interested but vague
- target fit is plausible but unclear
- wants updates but not clearly purchase-ready

### Weak signal
- generic curiosity only
- poor target fit
- no relevant pain point or intent

## Action rules
### Strong
- keep on early-access list
- note exact wording and pain point
- prepare follow-up when MVP is ready

### Medium
- keep on list
- note uncertainties
- use for messaging refinement

### Weak
- keep only if consent and purpose still fit
- avoid over-investing in interpretation

## Review rhythm
- review inbound interest in batches
- summarize patterns, not just individual emails
- look for repeated wording and repeated pain points
